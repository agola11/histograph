Histograph

A webapp that allows a user to understand his/her browsing habits in more depth. This is done by providing a series of graphs based on browsing history and generating recommendations for other webpages. Final Project for COS 333 at Princeton, taught by Brian Kernighan.

Introduction

Histograph is a web application that provides graphical analytics of a userâ€™s browsing history and recommends new websites for a user to browse based on their history and the history of their friends. The fundamental issue at hand is that users do not have a convenient means to understand their browsing history and do not have a way to adjust their browsing habits based on the browsing habits of their peers and others alike.

Brief Technical Overview

A chrome extension sends browsing history to a Django backend. Here, the data is cleaned, and parsed into URL tree data structures for each user. The trees are rendered into formats accepted by the client-side D3.js graphing library. Collaborative filtering algorithms are run on a user's and friends' trees to generate recommendations; each tree is traversed, and scores are calculated by taking into account similarity of distributions. Caching and pagination were used to improve performance. For more detail see our presentation or codebase. Also, feel free to email me!
